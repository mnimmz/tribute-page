Reverend Vandroth Backus

1925 - 2016

Biography

Reverend Vandroth Backus was known for many great things, including being one of the first Black police officers in Florence, South Carolina, as well as serving in the United States Marines, being part of a select group trained out of Montford Point, North Carolina, who helped desegregate the military. Backus also was employed as an educator in Darlington County for 14 years, was a member of the board of directors at Morris College and a Florence County councilman. When he retired from teaching, he entered into the ministry and founded the Monumental Missionary Baptist Church in Florence and the Backus Funeral Home. He was very devoted to his community as well as his family. Vandroth Backus passed away on December 26, 2016. He is survived by his wife, Florence, and three children. As his great niece, I truly appreciate his legacy.

Timeline of Reverend Vandroth Backus' Life

  • January 5, 1925 - Born in Florence, South Carolina.
  • 1947 - Graduated from Wilson High School in Florence and then attended the Renouard School of Embalming where he graduated that same year.
  • August 28, 1943 - Entered into the United States Marine Corps.
  • June 1, 1950 - Reverend Vandroth Backus and Reverend Solomon Eaddy,Sr. were the first Blacks hired to the Florence police force.
  • 1977 - Founded Backus Funeral Home
  • 2008 - Founded Monumental Missionary Baptist Church in Florence, South Carolina
  • June 8, 2014 - Oakland Avenue toward Norfolk Street was renamed “Rev. Dr. Vandroth Backus Way” to honor Backus.
  • October 15, 2014 - Presented with the Congressional Gold Medal for his military service by U.S. Rep. Jim Clyburn, D-S.C.
  • December 26, 2016 - Passed away at McLeod Hospice following a brief illness.
